#### 3.2 Hidden Conditions & Real-World Issues (Verified)

**🚨 Issue 1 — Utility MCC Tagging Inconsistency** `[Real-World Issue — Technofino/DesiDime confirmed]`

When you pay utility bills via PhonePe, the transaction can be tagged under two different merchant names depending on the payment routing:

| Statement Tag | What You Earn | Why |
|---|---|---|
| `UTILITY PP FSS IN` | **10% ✅** | Correctly tagged as PhonePe utility category |
| `WWWPHONEPECOMIN` | **1% only ❌** | Miscoded as generic PhonePe web transaction |

This is not a rare edge case — multiple users have reported inconsistency in the same billing cycle. SBI Card support has not provided a reliable resolution.

**Mitigation:** Always pay bills using **Card OTP mode** (not UPI mode) inside PhonePe. Card OTP payments consistently generate the correct `UTILITY PP FSS IN` tagging. UPI-mode payments via PhonePe for utilities have a higher chance of being miscoded.

**🚨 Issue 2 — PhonePe Platform Fee** `[Real-World Issue — DesiDime confirmed]`

PhonePe charges **₹3 per biller** for bill payments, regardless of payment mode. For your 4 electricity bills + gas + internet = 6 bills/month: **₹3 × 6 = ₹18/month = ₹216/year** quietly eating into rewards.

Additionally, if you pay utility bills via **UPI mode**, PhonePe charges a **2.1% convenience fee**. On ₹13,800 that's ₹289/month — which completely destroys the 10% reward. **Always use Card OTP mode.**

**🚨 Issue 3 — Shared PhonePe Category Cap**

All PhonePe-tier spends — utilities, insurance, recharges, travel — share **one 2,000 pt pool per month**. In your insurance payment month:

- Utility 10% on ₹13,800 = 1,380 pts
- Insurance 10% on ₹31,000 = 3,100 pts
- Combined = 4,480 pts → **capped at 2,000**

You earn ₹2,000 that month regardless of total spend. Strategy: Pay insurance first, then utilities in the same billing cycle.

**🚨 Issue 4 — Biller Must Be Listed on PhonePe**

Utilities paid outside PhonePe earn only 1% capped at **500 pts/month** — not the 10% rate. If any of your 4 electricity boards isn't on PhonePe's biller system, that specific bill earns only 1%.

**Mitigation:** Check all 4 boards on PhonePe before applying. CESC and WBSEDCL are generally supported but individual sub-boards may not be. If a biller is missing, route that specific bill through Tata Neu (Tata Pay) for 5%.

**Issue 5 — Reward Points Forfeited on Inactivity**

If the card has zero transactions for 13 consecutive months, all accumulated points are forfeited. Keep the card active with at least one small monthly transaction.

## 💸 Section 6 — Earning Cashback on Credit Card Bill Payments

A common question: *can you earn rewards when paying your own credit card bill?*

### What Doesn't Work (Earning ON the Card You're Paying)

**Every Indian credit card, without exception, earns ₹0 when paying its own bill.** The SBI Black T&C explicitly lists "credit card bill payments" in its exclusion table. Credit card bill payments are tagged as MCC 6051 (quasi-cash / financial institution) which is blocked from earning across all issuers. Paying Card A's bill using Card B is also blocked — same MCC tagging applies.

### What Does Work (App-Level Bonus on Top)

Third-party platforms give small rewards from their own budgets when you settle bills through them. These don't come from SBI Black or HSBC — they're the app's own promotion.

| Platform | What You Get | Realistic Annual Value on ~₹25k/mo bill |
|---|---|---|
| **CRED** | CRED Coins per payment, redeemable on Zomato/hotels/Cult.fit. ₹200 cashback on first payment. | **₹300–₹750/year** if you use CRED partner brands |
| **PhonePe** | Scratch cards — mystery cashback ₹0–₹50 per payment | **₹60–₹600/year** (random, unreliable) |
| **Google Pay** | Occasional personalised cashback offers | **₹0–₹100 occasionally** |
| **Paytm** | Scratch cards up to 1 lakh Paytm Points (1L pts = ₹1,000) — minimum 1,000 pts per transaction | **₹120–₹240/year** realistically |
| **Total realistic add-on** | | **₹400–₹1,200/year** |

### ⚠️ Critical Warning — Don't Pay Utilities via CRED Expecting SBI Black 10%

If you pay utility bills through CRED Bill Pay and charge it to SBI Black:
- CRED processes the payment — it does NOT go through PhonePe
- SBI Black earns **1% only** (base tier), not 10%
- You lose ₹1,300+/month in utility rewards

**Correct routing:** Pay utilities via **PhonePe App → Card OTP mode → SBI Black** to get 10%. Then pay the SBI Black bill via CRED for the CRED coins bonus.

### Best Practice

1. Pay **all utility bills** via PhonePe App (Card OTP) using SBI Black → 10%
2. Pay **SBI Black bill** via CRED → earn CRED coins as a bonus layer
3. Pay **HSBC Live+ bill** via PhonePe/GPay → scratch card bonus
4. Never reverse the order expecting PhonePe/CRED utility payment to earn 10% on SBI Black

## 👨‍👩‍👧 Section 7 — Family/Friend Card Sharing & Income Tax

### Add-On Cards for Family — Bank-Approved ✅

SBI Black allows up to 3 add-on cards for family members — spouse, parents, children/siblings above 18. All spend reports under your PAN. All liability is yours. All rewards accrue to your account. This is the correct legal method for family sharing.

### Sharing with Friends — ⚠️ Not Recommended

Sharing your primary card with a friend violates bank T&Cs, creates fraud liability for you, and increases your SFT-reported spend under your PAN alone. The correct model: friend transfers money to you via UPI first, you pay their bill on your card, you keep the reward. This creates a clean audit trail and no gift tax exposure.

### Income Tax Triggers — Your Profile

| Rule | Threshold | Your Status | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| SFT auto-reporting to IT Dept | **₹10 lakh/year credit card spend** | ~₹4.7L — safely under ✅ | None needed |
| Mandatory ITR filing — electricity bill | **₹1 lakh/year** | ₹1.44L — **already crossed** ⚠️ | Must file ITR every year |
| Cash credit card repayment reporting | **₹1 lakh cash** | Always pay digitally | Never repay in cash |
| Gift tax — friend's bills via your card | **₹50,000/year** tax-free to non-relatives | Friend's bills > ₹50k/year = taxable for them | Use the UPI reimbursement model |
| Income vs spend mismatch scrutiny | No hard limit — proportionality check | Your ₹4.7L spend should match declared income | Maintain accurate ITR |

**Your electricity bill alone (₹1.44L/year) already crosses the mandatory ITR filing threshold under Section 139(1). You must be filing ITR regardless of credit card spend.**

### AIS Matching — Action Required

Credit card transactions reflect in AIS, TIS, and Form 26AS on the income tax portal. Log into incometaxindia.gov.in, check your AIS portal, and verify that all credit card spends appearing there match what you've declared.
