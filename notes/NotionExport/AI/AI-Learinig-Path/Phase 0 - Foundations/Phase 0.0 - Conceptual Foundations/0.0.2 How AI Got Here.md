## 🗺️ Stage 0 — Concept Map

"**Why transformers?**" is a classic architect-interview question — this lesson is the answer. It's
a short history from the old sequential models to today's agents. It explains *why* modern tools
(context windows, the encoder/decoder split in the next lesson) exist at all.

## 🔑 New Terms (plain English)

- **RNN / LSTM** — older models that read text **one word at a time** (sequential, slow).
- **Transformer** — the 2017 architecture that reads **all words at once**; powers every modern LLM.
- **Attention** — a mechanism letting each word "look at" every other word to understand context.
- **Parallelisable** — splittable across thousands of GPUs at once (why transformers scale).
- **RLHF** — a training step using **human feedback** to make a model helpful and safe (it created ChatGPT).

## 🎈 Stage 1 — The Simple Idea (analogy: a straw vs. the whole page)

Old models (RNNs) read text as if **through a straw** — one word at a time, left to right — and by
the end of a long sentence they'd half-forgotten the start. The **transformer** instead sees the
**whole page at once** and lets every word "pay attention" to every other word. That single change
made models understand context far better *and* train far faster.

**The "Aha!":** the breakthrough wasn't just "more data" — it was an **architecture** (the
transformer) that reads everything simultaneously and scales across thousands of GPUs.

## ⚙️ Stage 2 — How It Actually Works (the timeline)

- **Pre-2017 — RNNs / LSTMs:** read left-to-right, sequential and slow; forgot long-range context
  ("vanishing gradient"); couldn't be parallelised. *Safely ignore for modern work* — but know why
  we left them.
- **2017 — "Attention Is All You Need" (Google):** the **transformer**. Core insight: look at all
  words at once and compute how much each word should attend to every other. Example: *"The bank by
  the river overflowed"* — attend "bank" → "river" (not "money"). It won because it (1) parallelises
  across GPUs, (2) handles long-range context, (3) keeps getting smarter as you add data + compute.
- **2018–2022 — the family split:** **BERT** (encoder-only — *understands* text → search,
  embeddings) vs **GPT** (decoder-only — *generates* text). GPT-3 (2020, 175B) showed surprising
  reasoning. **InstructGPT** (2022) added **RLHF** → this became ChatGPT.
- **2022–2026 — ChatGPT to agents:** ChatGPT (Nov 2022) → GPT-4, Claude, open-weight Llama/Mistral
  (2023) → GPT-4o multimodal + RAG becomes standard (2024) → reasoning models (o1/o3) + the MCP
  protocol (2025) → **agentic AI mainstream** (2026).

## 🚀 Stage 3 — In Practice / Why It Matters

Every model you'll touch in Phases 1–4 is a **transformer** — which is *why* context windows exist
and *why* hallucination happens. The encoder/decoder split introduced here decides which model you
use at each stage of a RAG pipeline (next lesson).

## 🧠 Common Misconceptions

- **"AI appeared with ChatGPT."** → The transformer (2017) and decades of ML led here; ChatGPT was
  just the consumer moment.
- **"Transformers are better only because they're bigger."** → They're better because they
  **parallelise** and capture **long-range context**; size *amplifies* that advantage.
- **"Modern LLMs still use RNNs."** → No — they're transformers. Know RNNs only so you can explain
  why the field moved on.

## 📌 Quick Reference (key facts)

- **2017 transformer = the turning point** (the "attention" idea).
- **encoder (BERT) understands · decoder (GPT) generates.**
- **RLHF** turned a raw model into a helpful chatbot (ChatGPT).
- everything since = **scaling + multimodal + reasoning + agents**.

> 🎯 **Interview angle:** "Why transformers over RNNs?" → parallelisable across GPUs, capture
> long-range context without forgetting, and scale with compute/data.

## 🛑 STOP — Self-Check

Give **two reasons** the industry moved from RNNs to transformers.

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

(1) **Parallelism** — RNNs process words one-at-a-time (sequential, slow, hard to scale), while
transformers process all words simultaneously and run across thousands of GPUs. (2) **Long-range
context** — RNNs forgot earlier words in long inputs, whereas attention lets every word directly
reference every other word, so meaning isn't lost over distance. (A bonus third: transformers keep
improving as you add data and compute.)
</details>
